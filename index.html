Storage and safety tips for every Filipino bread
🌡️ General Rules
☀️
Philippine heat & humidity
At 30°C+ with 80%+ humidity, mold can appear on soft bread within 24–36 hours. Store away from sunlight and stoves.
❄️
Freeze, not fridge (plain breads)
Refrigerators dry out plain bread. Freeze instead — pauses staling completely. Only refrigerate filled breads.
🛍️
Paper bag vs plastic bag
Paper bags let bread breathe. Never seal warm bread in plastic — condensation causes mold within hours.
🕐
2-hour rule for filled breads
Pan de Coco, Kalihim, Inipit — no more than 2 hours at room temperature. Bacteria multiply fastest at 20°C–40°C.
🚫
Never eat moldy bread
Mold on soft bread means spores have already spread through the entire loaf. Discard the whole piece immediately.
